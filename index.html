The Championships at Wimbledon

What is Wimbledon?

Wimbledon is one of the four Grand Slam tournaments in professional tennis, along with the French Open, the Austrlian Open, and the US Open. Wimbledon is located at the All England Club in Wimbledon, London. All of the tennis courts at the club are grass, making it the only Grand Slam that is played on grass. Normally the tournament takes place from mid-June to early July, but because of the coronavirus the 2020 tournament was cancelled.

How Did Wimbledon Begin?

The first championship at Wimbledon began was in 1877. The tournament was organized because the club (which at the time was called the All England Croquet and Lawn Tennis Club) needed a new pony roller to maintain their lawns for croquet. The tournament was only for men's singles and featured a field of 22 players. After the tournament, the club made some adjustments and began holding the tournament annualy.
